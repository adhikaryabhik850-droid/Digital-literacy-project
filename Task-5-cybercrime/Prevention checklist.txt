1. Always verify the sender’s email address before clicking on any link, especially for internships or job offers.

2. Never share your OTP, passwords, or bank details with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank or official authority.

3. Avoid clicking on unknown or shortened links (like bit.ly) received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email.

4. Use strong and unique passwords for different accounts, and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.

5. Do not log in to important accounts (banking, email) using public Wi-Fi networks.

6. For UPI safety: Never approve unknown payment requests or “collect requests” in apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.

7. For UPI safety: Remember that receiving money does NOT require entering your UPI PIN — only sending money does.

8. Always download apps only from official sources like Google Play Store or Apple App Store.

9. Regularly update your phone and apps to ensure the latest security protections are active.

10. Be cautious of messages creating urgency (e.g., “limited time offer”, “account will be blocked”) — take time to verify before acting.

11. Log out of shared devices and avoid saving passwords on public or borrowed systems.

12. Report any suspicious activity immediately on:
   - National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
   - Helpline Number: 1930
